Education

M.Sc. in Computer Science, Stony Brook University

Thesis: Checkpoint/Restart Implementation for OpenSHMEM - My thesis was focused on making OpenSHMEM, a PGAS parallel programming library, resilient towards failures. As the most popular resilience method, I implemented an in-memory checkpoint/restart system for OpenSHMEM. In this system, processes (the parallel entities) can request for their data to be saved in memory and when a failure happens, the last saved data will be reloaded in all the processes. If a process is dead, it will be replaced with a new process. Advisor: Prof. Barbara Chapman.

Relevant Coursework: Algorithms, Parallel Programming Models, Fundamentals of Computer Networks, Artificial Intelligence, Theory of Database Systems, System Security.

B.Sc. in Computer Engineering, Sharif University of Technology

The test and train data was derived from 19 movies and labeled automatically using the subtitles. Advisor: Dr. Hossein Sameti.

Other Projects and Research Experiences

Parallel Computing: I joined the Exascallab group led by Prof. Barbara Chapman in Jan 2017 and I have worked on High Performance Computing, especially Parallel Programming ever since. My main project has been around fault tolerance in OpenSHMEM and MPI. For more infor, see my education.

Web Application Firewall (WAF): A project for System Security course (Fall 2016)- we implemented a WAF including multiple systems such as a packet analyzer, a URL parser, and train and test modes. The WAF was designed to stop XSS attacks and SQL injections on a Joomla weblog (using a vulnerable version). After arrival of a packet at the packet analyzer, the reuqested URL was parsed to detect the subparts of the URL and type of request. In training mode (when we suppose all requests are legitimate), we stored the URL subparts and the type of request made presented by a bit vector (e.g., if the request only consists of alpha-numerical characters) in nested hashtables. At the end of the training mode, all stored data was flushed to a file to be used during test mode as a white list. During test mode, the file was loaded back into hashtables and any packet with a request outside of the white list was dropped. I implemented the store and search process with hashtables in C.

Handwritten Digit Recognition: A project for Machine Learning course (Spring 2016)- we compared the accuracy of different Machine Learning models (e.g. K-NN, K-means, and SVM) along with Image Processing methods (such as edge, line or corner detectors, Hough Transform, etc) to categorize pictures of low-resolution handwritten digits. We achieved up to 94% accuracy - programmed with MATLAB.

Graph Labeling and Factorization: (Spring 2014 - Spring 2015) We studied graph labeling and chromatic number of a special group of graphs (forests), developed and proved new theories and tested some using C++ programming. This research was in collaboration with Graph Theory Group of Institute for Research in Fundamental Science (IPM).

GUI for Data Search: (Spring 2014) - During internship for ASR Gooyesh Pardaz, I implemented a GUI using Java Swing enabling the user to search for data with desired labels (e.g. length, male or female voices, year, etc) in text and speech dataset.

Automatic Language Detection: A project for the graduate course Speech Processing (Spring 2014) - We developed a program to detect the language of speech files in English, French, and German, with MATLAB. We tried implementing different methods and I implemented the train and test parts using SVM method and achieved more than 90% accuracy.

Whole Transcriptome Assembly: A project for the graduate course Fundamentals of Bioinformatics (Spring 2014) - We were given the sequences of rice genome (Oryza Sativa species), using the sequences of Harvard Gene Index, UTRdb, RAP, and some other databases, to analyze in order to increase the length of the sequences and decrease the error, programmed in C++.

Camera Calibration: An individual project for 3D Computer Vision course (Spring 2014) - calibrating a camera using a set of pictures of the same object from different angles and different camera properties - programmed with MATLAB.

Games: Two individual projects for Advanced Programming (Java) course (Fall 2011) - implemented Tetris and one board game, using Java Swing and Object Oriented Programming.

Hobbies

Journalism

Starting from the end of high school, reading and thinking about women's right became an enormous interest of mine. After entering my undergraduate university, I found the oppurtunity to join groups with focus on women's rights and studies, which soon became my biggest extracurricular activity. Living in one of countries with the least gender equality, I made it my mission to increase my own knowledge, awareness, and movements inside the university towards a better place for female students. Due to large effects of politics in Iran on gender issues, I also became interested in the politics and everyday local and global news. Some of my more significant activities were:

Women's Rights and Studies Groups: During my undergraduate years 2010-2015, I joined three different women’s rights and studies groups. During weekly or bi-weekly meetings, we read and discussed different articles and books about women's rights and issues, and the politics and religous beliefs around them. We celebrated Women's International Day (not observed in Iran), and updated the clubs' boards frequently.

Digari Journal (گاهنامه‌ی دیگری): The journal's name, meaning "The Other", was inspired by de Beauvoir’s quote in The Second Sex, “He is the Subject, he is the Absolute – she is the Other.” I was the chief editor of this cultural-social journal, and we wrote articles with focus on women and minorities' problems in Iran. My articles mostly covered violence against women and hijab enforcement inside the university which had intensified during those years.

Khabarname Weekly Journal (هفته‌نامه‌ی خبرنامه): Around 2015, I started to write for the women’s right section of another weekly student journal, called “Khabarname”, or “News Letter”. Aside from a special article for International Women’s Day, my most significant work was one criticizing the government’s lack of attention to and work on women’s issues. This journal was the most popular journal at the time in the university, selling a few hundred copies each week.

RAA: After coming to US, in 2018 a friend of mine started an online radio for Persian speaking immigrants of North America, called RAA. Most of this radio’s programs were produced by Iranian students in US, and I started a group for a newscasting program. We, a group of four, gathered the world’s (mostly Iran and US) news from multiple sources, picked up to 15 subjects and wrote short reports on them, recorded them three times a week, edited the records for higher quality and less noise, and broadcasted them on the radio’s website and mobile applications. In less than a year, the radio gained more than a hundred listeners worldwide.
