About Me

I am an Iranian computer scientist living in U.S. I achieved my M.Sc. in Computer Science at Stony Brook University, and my B.Sc. in Computer Engineering at Shaif University of Technology. Currently, I am looking for jobs and my interests are algorithms and problem solving, parallel programming, web development, and anything creative. I like to work in a creative, cooperative and instructive environment. If interested, contact me at dmirfendereski [at] gmail [dot] com.

Education

M.Sc. in Computer Science, Stony Brook University

Thesis: Checkpoint/Restart Implementation for OpenSHMEM - My thesis was focused on making OpenSHMEM, a PGAS parallel programming library, resilient towards failures. As the most popular resilience method, I implemented an in-memory checkpoint/restart system for OpenSHMEM. In this system, processes (the parallel entities) can request for their data to be saved in memory and when a failure happens, the last saved data will be reloaded in all the processes. If a process is dead, it will be replaced with a new process. Advisor: Prof. Barbara Chapman.

Relevant Coursework: Algorithms, Parallel Programming Models, Fundamentals of Computer Networks, Artificial Intelligence, Theory of Database Systems, System Security.

B.Sc. in Computer Engineering, Sharif University of Technology

Thesis: Speech/Non-Speech Recognition - In my thesis, I developed a program with MATLAB to detect speech or non-speech parts of audio files using SVM with linear kernel, and achieved up to 95\% accuracy. The test and train data was derived from 19 movies and labeled automatically using the subtitles. Advisor: Dr. Hossein Sameti.

Relevant Coursework: Advanced Programming (Java), Data Structures and Algorithms, Web Development (Audited), 3D Computer Vision, Speech Processing, Fundamentals of Bioinformatics.

Hobbies

Journalism

Starting from the end of high school, reading and thinking about women's right became an enormous interest of mine. After entering m undergraduate university, I found the oppurtunity to join groups with focus on women's rights and studies, which soon became my biggest extracurricular activity. Living in one of countries with the least gender equality, I made it my mission to increase my own knowledge, awareness, and movements inside the university towards a better place for female students. Due to large effects of politics in Iran on women's rights, I also became interested in the politics and everyday news in Iran and the World. Some of my more significant activities were:

Women Rights and Studies Groups: During my undergraduate years 2010-2015, I joined three different women’s rights and studies groups. During weekly or bi-weekly meetings, we read and discussed different articles and books about women's rights and issues, and the politics and religous beliefs around them. We celebrated Women's International Day (not observed in Iran), and updated the clubs' boards frequently.

Digari Journal (گاهنامه‌ی دیگری): The journal's name, meaning "The Other", was inspired by de Beauvoir’s quote in The Second Sex, “He is the Subject, he is the Absolute – she is the Other.” I was the chief editor of this cultural-social journal, and we wrote articles with focus on women and minorities' problems in Iran. My articles mostly covered violence against women and hijab enforcement inside the university which had intensified during those years.

Khabarname Weekly Journal (هفته‌نامه‌ی خبرنامه): Around 2015, I started to write for the women’s right section of another weekly journal, called “Khabarname”, or “News Letter”. Aside from a special article for International Women’s Day, my most significant work was one criticizing the government’s lack of attention to women’s problems. This journal was the most popular journal at the time in the university, selling a few hundred copies each week.

RAA: After coming to US, in 2018 a friend of mine started an online radio for Persian speaking immigrants of North America, called RAA. Most of this radio’s programs were produced by Iranian students in US, and I started a group for a newscasting program. We, a group of four, gathered the world’s (mostly Iran and US) news from multiple sources, picked up to 15 subjects and wrote short reports on them, recorded them three times a week, edited the records for higher quality and less noise, and broadcasted them on the radio’s website and mobile applications. In less than a year, the radio gained more than a hundred listeners worldwide.
